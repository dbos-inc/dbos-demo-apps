Build reliable software effortlessly

DBOS adds durable execution to your applications, checkpointing their workflows and steps to Postgres. If your apps ever crash, DBOS recovers them from their last completed steps.

Try it: launch a workflow, then crash this app. When you restart, the workflow always resumes from exactly where it left off.

app/main.py 
@DBOS.workflow()def workflow():step_one()step_two()step_three() @DBOS.step()def step_one():time.sleep(5)DBOS.logger.info("Completed step 1!") @DBOS.step()def step_two():time.sleep(5)DBOS.logger.info("Completed step 2!") @DBOS.step()def step_three():time.sleep(5)DBOS.logger.info("Completed step 3!")

DBOS durable workflows can be started on a cron-style schedule. The schedule can be adjusted while the app is running. It may take up to a minute for changes to propagate.

app/main.py 
@DBOS.workflow()def scheduled_workflow(scheduled_time, context):DBOS.logger.info("Scheduled workflow starting.")DBOS.sleep(1)DBOS.logger.info("Scheduled workflow ending.") DBOS.apply_schedules([{"schedule_name": "scheduled-workflow","workflow_fn": scheduled_workflow,"schedule": "*/5 * * * * *",}])

DBOS queues let you run workflow fan-outs and control concurrency. The worker_concurrency parameter controls how many workflows one process may run simultaneously. You can adjust it without restarting the app. Press "Enqueue Workflow" repeatedly to put workflows on the queue.

app/main.py 
@DBOS.workflow()def enqueued_workflow():DBOS.logger.info("Enqueued workflow starting.")DBOS.sleep(5)DBOS.logger.info("Enqueued workflow ending.") DBOS.register_queue("demo-queue",worker_concurrency=3) DBOS.enqueue_workflow("demo-queue",enqueued_workflow)

DBOS workflows can receive messages while running, enabling inter-workflow communication and human-in-the-loop patterns. DBOS.recv() durably waits for a message, surviving crashes and restarts. Here we use DBOS.send() to deliver a decision from an endpoint.

app/main.py 
@DBOS.workflow()def communication_workflow():comm_step_one()DBOS.set_event("comm_status", "waiting")decision = DBOS.recv("approval",    timeout_seconds=120)if decision == "approve":comm_step_two()elif decision == "deny":DBOS.logger.info("Denied.")else:  # timeoutDBOS.logger.info("Timed out!")
